01 — What Is Solar

Energy Born
from Light

Solar energy is the radiation produced by the Sun and harnessed using a range of technologies — from photovoltaic cells converting light to electricity, to solar thermal collectors generating heat.

The Sun delivers more energy to Earth in one hour than humanity consumes in an entire year. It is the most abundant, inexhaustible, and cleanest energy source available to our civilization.

Unlike fossil fuels formed over millions of years, solar energy is perpetually renewable — available every single day, everywhere on Earth, with zero fuel cost and zero carbon emissions.

Renewable Zero Emissions Inexhaustible Scalable Distributed Low Maintenance

02 — How It Works

The Journey of
a Photon

01
☀️

Sunlight Arrives

Photons — particles of light — travel 150 million km from the Sun to Earth in about 8 minutes, carrying electromagnetic energy in the form of radiation.

02
⚛️

Photovoltaic Effect

When photons strike a silicon solar cell, they knock electrons loose. The cell's built-in electric field forces these free electrons into directional current — electricity.

03
🔄

DC to AC Conversion

An inverter converts the raw direct current (DC) from panels into alternating current (AC) — the standard form used by homes, businesses, and the electrical grid.

04
🏠

Power Your World

Clean electricity flows to power appliances, charge devices, heat water, and run vehicles — or is stored in batteries or fed back to the grid for credit.

03 — Types of Solar

Three Ways to
Capture the Sun

Most Common

Photovoltaic (PV)

Silicon-based solar panels that directly convert sunlight into electricity via the photovoltaic effect. Found on rooftops, solar farms, and consumer devices worldwide.

Monocrystalline cells achieve the highest efficiency, while thin-film panels offer flexibility for unconventional surfaces.

29% Peak Commercial Efficiency
Residential & Industrial

Solar Thermal

Collectors that absorb sunlight to directly heat water or air for residential hot water systems, space heating, pool heating, and industrial process heat.

Evacuated tube collectors perform even in cold or cloudy climates, making them viable globally.

70% Thermal Efficiency Possible
Utility Scale

Concentrated Solar (CSP)

Mirrors or lenses focus sunlight onto a receiver to generate extreme heat, which drives turbines. CSP can store thermal energy for electricity generation after dark.

Tower systems can reach temperatures above 1,000°C for industrial-scale power generation.

565°C Operating Temperature

04 — Benefits

Why Solar Wins

🌍

Zero Carbon Emissions

Solar panels produce no greenhouse gases during operation. Over their lifetime, they offset hundreds of tonnes of CO₂ compared to fossil fuel alternatives — a direct climate benefit.

💰

Dramatically Falling Costs

Solar electricity costs have dropped over 90% since 2010 — from $0.28 to under $0.03 per kWh in the best markets. It is now the cheapest electricity source in history.

⚡

Energy Independence

Homes and businesses with solar generation reduce or eliminate grid dependency. Nations with high solar adoption reduce dependence on imported fossil fuels, strengthening energy security.

💼

Massive Job Creation

The solar industry employs over 4 million people worldwide — in manufacturing, installation, maintenance, and research — with rapid growth projected through 2030 and beyond.

🔇

Silent & Minimal Footprint

Rooftop solar requires no additional land and produces no noise. Ground-mounted systems can co-exist with agriculture (agrivoltaics), doubling land use efficiency.

🌐

Universal Accessibility

Solar is deployable anywhere — from remote villages to city skyscrapers. Off-grid solar has already brought electricity to over 420 million people who lacked grid access.

07 — The Future

What's Coming
in Solar

NOW — 2027

Perovskite Solar Cells

Next-generation solar cells using perovskite crystals promise efficiencies above 35% at drastically lower manufacturing costs than traditional silicon — potentially revolutionizing solar economics.

2025 — 2030

Solar + Battery Integration

Paired storage systems make solar viable 24/7, eliminating intermittency. Grid-scale battery projects are already powering cities through night and cloudy periods reliably.

2030 — 2040

Building-Integrated PV

Solar roof tiles, transparent solar glass windows, and solar facades will turn every building surface into a power generator — invisible integration into the urban landscape.

2040 — BEYOND

Space-Based Solar Power

Satellites collecting solar energy in orbit — where the Sun shines 24/7 at full intensity — and beaming it wirelessly to Earth could deliver virtually limitless clean power.
